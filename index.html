How Your Data Is Stored
🔒 100% Local Storage
Everything you enter in this app — your resume, the job description, interviewer profiles, and your API key — is stored exclusively in your browser's localStorage. It never touches any server. It never leaves your device.

What this means for you:
• Your data persists between sessions on the same browser and device
• Clearing your browser data or switching browsers will clear the app
• No account is needed and nothing is synced to any cloud
• If you use an AI API key, your answer text is sent to the provider you chose (Anthropic or OpenAI) for feedback — that's the only time data leaves your device
• Your API key is stored locally and sent only to the provider's API endpoint

How the Practice Simulator Works

This simulator lets you practice answering interview questions out loud, then get immediate feedback on your response. Here's how to use it:

① Select
Pick an interviewer from your profile list, then choose a question they're likely to ask.
② Record
Click the mic button and speak your answer. Your words will appear as you talk. Click again to stop.
③ Feedback
Click "Get Feedback" to receive coaching. With an API key, feedback is persona-driven. Without one, you'll get STARR-based best-practice coaching.
④ Export
Export individual sessions, or capture everything with Export Full Report (available in the Interview Setup tab).
Browser compatibility & storage limit
Voice recording uses your browser's built-in speech recognition — it works best in Chrome or Edge. Firefox and Safari have limited support. You can also type your answer directly in the transcript box if you prefer.

Practice sessions are saved locally, up to 20 most recent. Once you hit 20, the oldest session is removed to make room for the new one. Export your Full Report regularly to preserve a complete record of your practice.

📘 Interview Best-Practice Playbook — the 5 tough questions & 10 sharp ones to ask
Nailing the 5 questions almost everyone gets
1 · “Tell me about yourself.”
Lead with the value you bring. Summarize your career in 2–3 sentences, land one signature win, and tie it to this role.
e.g. “I've spent seven years building programs that consistently doubled results. I'm excited to bring that momentum to a team like yours.”
2 · “What's your greatest weakness?”
Name a real, non-disqualifying growth area and the concrete steps you're taking on it.
e.g. “I used to hold onto too much myself. I've leaned into delegation with clear ownership and weekly check-ins — and the team ships faster for it.”
3 · “Why are you leaving your current role?”
Stay positive and future-focused. Talk toward what you want, not away from complaints.
e.g. “I've learned a lot and I'm ready for more ownership and impact — exactly what drew me to this role.”
4 · “Where do you see yourself in 5 years?”
Show growth that aligns with where the company is headed.
e.g. “Leading a team and owning strategy in this space — I see this role as a strong step toward that.”
5 · “Why should we hire you?”
Map your strengths to their needs, backed by measurable results.
e.g. “You need someone who can streamline operations. I cut processing time 40% last year and I'd love to do the same here.”
10 sharp questions to ask them
  1. “What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?” — shows you're outcome-focused.
  2. “What are the biggest challenges someone in this role will face?” — positions you as a proactive problem-solver.
  3. “How is success measured on the team?” — helps you understand how to exceed expectations.
  4. “Can you share a recent example of how the team supported someone's growth?” — reveals whether growth is real here.
  5. “What do new hires often find surprising once they start?” — surfaces the gap between perception and reality.
  6. “How does the team give and receive feedback?” — tells you about the culture.
  7. “If you could change one thing about how the team works, what would it be?” — surfaces pain points tactfully.
  8. “Can you tell me about a time the company's values shaped a hard decision?” — tests whether values are lived, not just posters.
  9. “How has the team evolved over the past year?” — signals adaptability and direction.
  10. “Is there anything I haven't asked that I should have?” — a thoughtful closer that earns extra insight.
Pick 3–4 that fit the conversation — you don't need all ten. And always keep truthful, specific examples ready (the STARR method in the coaching below helps structure them).
